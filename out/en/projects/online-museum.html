Online museum of ZHEK-art
Project reach 10 million

task
To rethink the role of ZHEK-art as a decorative and applied art and remove derogatory associations from it.
ideas and solutions
An interactive online museum where you can explore the subject and immerse yourself in the atmosphere of a real residential housing courtyard.

Swans made of tires, Cheburashka made of bottles, stumps with eyes — in the Bestiary section you can see the outlandish characters of ZHEK-art around you and get acquainted with their biographies.

Using the art constructor in the DIY section, you can create your own unique work of ZHEK-art: download any background and assemble the character from materials at hand.

The Online Museum of ZHEK-art is a large-scale
multidisciplinary project with bright typography, illustrations and 3D objects, animation and a 360-degree panorama on the main page. The general message of the project is that even against the background of gray reality, there can be a place for bright original creativity, accessible to everyone and worth appreciating.

As part of a large-scale PR campaign supporting the launch of the project, we collaborated with popular Internet artists: we modeled a set of LEGO — dedicated to Russian ZHEK-art — with Oleg Lego, printed a comic with Vitaly Terletsky, released a limited edition merch with Nikita Gruzovik, and published an AR mask on Instagram.
